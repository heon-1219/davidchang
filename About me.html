About me

Hi, my name is David. I'm an international(F-1) undergraduate student at the University of Texas at Austin. My current studies are Computer Science & Electrical and Computer Engineering. I'm interested in how computers function, with an equal amount of interest in software and hardware components. By further exploring computational functionalities and physical components that make it possible, I wish to make something of my own one day. My interdisciplinary and research interests include medical data and computational biology.

Over the summer, I participated in research at Ajou University Medical Center's Bio-Informatics Lab, where I got hands-on experience with using computational methods to analyze medical data. First semester of my sophomore year at UT Austin, I organized a team of 5 students to compete in UT Austin BioML Society's Bits to Binders challenge, where we were challenged to find a de novo protein design for CAR-T cell binding site using ML techniques.

After Spring 2025 semester, I will be serving my country as part of the Korean Augmentation to the United States Army(KATUSA) Soldier Program for 18 months.

Outside of school and responsibilities, I love to play the piano or spend time in the gym.
